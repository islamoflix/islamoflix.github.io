Who is Allah?

Allah is the arabic term of the word "THE GOD". What does that mean? Means when you want to call the god in arabic. You don't say god but rather you say the word "Allah". For example the arab christians still uses the word Allah.

And the similar words uses in the similar semitic languages. Like, aramaic which Isa or Jesus peace and blessings be upon him used to speak. The aramaic word for the god is "Alaha". Very similar right?

The word Allah can be break into two words al-ilah. Which is the exactly root word of the word 'Allah'.

Funfact! the arabic letters and some related semitic languages works like a tree. Where there are some root words and from these root words, the other other compiles based on the context.

Example:-

The word Qur'an means book. But quran is not a base word. It usually means something to read or something written. Which derived from the other root words. In fact there are some derived words which only appears in Qur'an which is usually not in use with normal arabic speakers.

The word ilaha means 'God' or something to worship. Because obviously god meant to be worshiped.

By just this little knowledge, we can understand the starting statement of the Shahadah. Which is 'la ilaha illallah'(there is no god but allah).
